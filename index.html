V1 Definition of Success

"The ability to link, organize, and intelligently query a large volume of information — retrieving smart responses that help PMs remember and process more information than a human brain can naturally. The tool enhances the PM's thought process, prioritizes information by highlighting key details, and links them to past views."
— Simon Sylvester, Rezco

What V1 IS

  • Enhanced memory for PMs — compensates for human bias
  • Qualitatively heavy research tool
  • Non-linear data linking and smart retrieval
  • Links current events to past PM views with timestamps
  • Flags inconsistencies in past thinking
  • Contrastive analysis (e.g., Shoprite vs. sector, not vs. gold mines)
  • Replaces and improves upon the analyst function

What V1 is NOT

  • Not a PM replacement — PMs are best at decisions
  • Not an AI-generated sell-side report (that's "beta")
  • Not quantitative — Bloomberg/Reuters feeds ditched
  • No portfolio review or construction
  • No AI personas or morning briefings
  • No automated model updates (possibly V2)
  • Not a feature-bloated v1 — lean and focused

V1 Data Ingestion Strategy

Three ingestion channels, prioritizing data quality over automation. Quantitative feeds (Bloomberg, Reuters) are out of scope.

Fireflies.ai MCP

P0 — Meetings

All PM meetings and calls recorded via Fireflies. The MCP server provides:

  • Full transcripts with speaker attribution
  • Per-sentence timestamps
  • AI-generated summaries and action items
  • Search across all meetings by keyword, date, participant
  • Read-only, account-scoped access via API key
Key tools: fireflies_search, fireflies_get_transcript, fireflies_get_summary, fireflies_fetch

Google Workspace MCP

P0 — Chat & Drive

Captures the PM conversation layer — the unstructured chat where Rob, Simon, and Sean share articles, charts, clips, and ideas daily.

  • Google Chat: search messages across spaces, retrieve conversations
  • Google Drive: search and retrieve uploaded PDFs, presentations
  • Gmail: access sell-side reports received via email
  • OAuth 2.0 authentication with token refresh
  • 100+ tools across 12 Google services
Server: google_workspace_mcp (taylorwilsdon) — covers Chat, Drive, Gmail, Calendar, Docs

Manual Upload

P0 — Curated Docs

Junior staff manually uploads curated documents to maintain data quality. No automated scraping.

  • Company earnings presentations (PDF)
  • Selected sell-side reports from trusted analysts
  • Annual reports — focus on "outlook & prospects" pages
  • Investor relations website documents
  • Each document tagged to asset/sector on upload
Why manual? Rob: "Essential for controlling data quality and content." Automated ingestion risks flooding the system with noise.

V1 Historical Data Approach

Exponential Decay (Default)

Most recent earnings reports are more relevant than older ones. The system weights recent data higher by default. Rob: "backward-looking exponential decay."

Latest Quarter
Highest Weight
1 Year Ago
Medium
3+ Years Ago
Low (unless queried)

Deep History (On-Demand)

Some queries require full history. Simon's example: "Has this CEO delivered on promises over 7 years?" The system must retain all data but surface it only when the query demands it.

User discipline matters: PMs must phrase queries carefully. The system should focus on easily digestible, relevant components — e.g., the one-page "outlook and prospects" from an annual report rather than the full 200-page document.

V1 Front-End: Two Components

Simon outlined exactly two interfaces for V1. Portfolio review, personas, dashboards — all reserved for later versions.

Workflow Manager

A thoughtful workflow management tool that proactively flags potential issues and incomplete work.

  • Price-vs-view alerts: "Share price dropped 15% since your positive discussion on [date]"
  • Stale view detection: "No update on Discovery in 3 months despite material developments"
  • Incomplete work flags: "You discussed X but never followed up"
  • Inconsistency detection: "You said Y was too expensive at R50, it's now R42"
  • Timestamp-aware: All flags include when the original view was expressed

Query Interface

A simple, Gemini-style querying tool for information retrieval. Natural language, smart responses.

  • Natural language queries: "What's our latest view on Shoprite?"
  • Contrastive analysis: "Compare Shoprite with its sector peers" (not with gold mines)
  • Temporal queries: "What did we discuss about FirstRand last quarter?"
  • Deep history: "Has Discovery's CEO delivered on promises over 5 years?"
  • Source-cited responses: Every answer links back to the meeting/document/chat
  • Cross-reference: Links internal views to external data automatically

Strategic Context

Rezco Asset Management (est. 2005, Johannesburg) is executing a radical shift: replacing the entire human analyst layer with an AI-powered research agent. The AI serves as an analyst, not a portfolio manager — it provides decision support to PMs Rob, Simon, and Sean.

The Problem

Portfolio Manager Bottleneck

  • PMs spend excessive time training and motivating junior analysts
  • High analyst churn rate wastes training investment
  • More human analysts = more management overhead, not more alpha
  • Human analysts are "bad filters" — strip out signals they perceive as noise

Information Fragmentation

  • Data scattered: Google Chat, Bloomberg, emails, meetings, calendars
  • No centralized logging of investment debates and decisions
  • Ideas stagnate in archives — incomplete work goes untracked
  • Geopolitical noise distracts from specific investment signals

The Solution: AI Analyst Agent

An orchestrator-based system of specialized AI sub-agents that ingest, map, analyze, and surface investment intelligence — grounded in Rezco's own philosophy.

What it does: Ingests all data sources, builds a non-linear knowledge graph ("Spiderweb"), provides prompted and unprompted analysis through multiple AI personas, tracks conviction over time, detects contradictions, and surfaces high-value signals.
What it does NOT do: Make buy/sell decisions. Generate alpha recommendations. Act as a portfolio manager. Operate as a black box. The PM remains the decision-maker.

Key Architectural Principles

  • Architecture at inception matters — biggest cost is PM time training the AI
  • Flexible foundation — add capabilities without rebuilding
  • Not a black box — sources cited, reasoning transparent
  • Reduce PM friction — every feature saves time, doesn't add it
  • Continuous, not episodic — state maintained across months/years
  • Proprietary vs. public always distinguishable — compliance requirement
  • Analyst, not PM — information and analysis only
  • Buy-side is different — always relative, cross-sector, never siloed
  • Benchmark-agnostic, not benchmark-ignorant
  • Signal over noise — amplify faint signals, filter the rest
  • Ideas must not die in the archive — active monitoring
  • Cognitive diversity by design — multiple AI personas

Headwinds/Tailwinds Framework

Core Rezco investment philosophy that the AI must deeply understand and apply.

Tailwind Environment

Positive factors outweigh negatives. Companies underpromise and overdeliver. Environment is "good and getting better." Management provides positive news incrementally.

Headwind Environment

Binary and high-risk. Management drip-feeds bad news. "It's tough out there," "consumers are struggling." Hedging bets. Hoping conditions improve.

CapabilityDescription
Longitudinal Earnings Call AnalysisDetect shifts in management tone over time — from bullish to defensive or vice versa
Linguistic Cue DetectionTrack specific phrases: "it's tough out there," "consumers are struggling," "we continue to see improvement"
H/T ClassifierRunning classification per company: net headwind, net tailwind, or transitioning
Inflection Point DetectionFind points where long-term projects "bear fruit" — not yet in financial models

Conviction Tracking

  • Record every PM view: timestamp, instrument, direction, confidence
  • Build conviction curve per instrument per PM
  • Surface when conviction changed without acknowledgment

Contradiction Detection

  • Current views vs. historical views on same instrument
  • Views vs. portfolio positioning ("worried about SA macro but overweight retailers")
  • Management statements vs. financial results
  • Stated intentions vs. actual execution

Current Orchestrator Flow (E2E)

Browser (ChatWidget.vue) → Console A2A (gRPC) → Orchestrator (ADK Agent) → Streaming Response

Orchestrator responsibilities: A2A protocol, ADK agent graph, session/task management, streaming events. Internal state in separate Spanner tables (Sessions, SessionEvents, Tasks).
